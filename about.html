HER ROOTS

My Nani, Pritam, affectionately known as Gudi (because she looked like a doll growing up!), has a story that begins in Punjab. She was born there before moving to Malaysia as a baby during the partition of Pakistan. As the eldest of seven siblings, she grew up in the vibrant town of Malacca, nurturing her love for cooking amidst a bustling household. After marrying my grandfather, she moved to Petaling Jaya, where she continues to reside.

A dedicated English school teacher, Nani inspired countless students across secondary schools in Malacca Town. Beyond the classroom, she has a passion for gardening, sewing, and storytelling. Her vibrant garden is a testament to her green thumb, while her beautifully crafted clothes and captivating tales reflect her creativity and warmth. Nani's big heart, selflessness, and generosity shine through in everything she does. Through her cooking, she keeps the rich traditions of her Punjabi heritage alive, sharing her love and passion with every dish she prepares.

THE FAMILY

Nani's heart and home are the center of our family. She has three children, who have blessed her with eight grandchildren. Nani played a crucial role in raising every one of us from when we were babies, creating a close-knit family bound by love and shared traditions.
