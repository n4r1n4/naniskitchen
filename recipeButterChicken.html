Butter Chicken
TIME: 2 hours SERVINGS: 4 SPECIAL EQUIPMENT: saucepan, oven
Directions
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger
  • 1 lemon
  • 2-3 cloves garlic
  • 1 ½ pounds (about 2-3) boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 4 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp chili powder or paprika
  • 1 tsp fenugreek
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup heavy cream (sub for half & half or yogurt for low fat)
Instructions
  1. If using bone-in chicken thighs, make 2 to 3 deep gashes in the chicken; skip this step if using boneless pieces.
  2. Place all the ingredients for the marinade in a medium bowl and combine until smooth. Fold in the chicken, ensuring it is completely coated with the marinade. Leave in the refrigerator covered for 1 hour.
  3. When ready to cook, preheat the oven to 200°C.
  4. Lay the marinated chicken pieces out on a baking dish or roasting pan. Roast the chicken in the oven until the internal temperature reaches 74°C, about 30 to 45 minutes, rotating the dish halfway through during cooking.
  5. Transfer the baking dish to the upper rack of the oven, and broil over high for 2 to 4 minutes, until the chicken starts to char in a few spots. Remove the baking dish from the oven.
  6. At the 20-minute mark of cooking the chicken, start the sauce. Melt the butter in a saucepan over low heat.
  7. Once the butter begins to foam, stir in the oil. Add the chili powder, toasted cumin, garlic, ginger, garam masala, and green chili. Stir until fragrant, 30 to 45 seconds.
  8. Add the tomato paste and cook until the paste begins to turn light brown, 5 to 6 minutes.
  9. Pour in the heavy cream and add the chicken along with any liquids left behind in the baking dish.
  10. Stir in the lemon juice, taste, and season with salt. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat; the fat should separate from the sauce.
  11. Remove from the heat. Garnish with cilantro and salted butter if using. Serve hot or warm with rice or naan.
